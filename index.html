About Me

After graduating in 2015 with a first class masters in physics I set my self the goal to pursue a career in physics. I successfully gained employment with QinetiQ (one of the UK's leading defence companies) as a Radio Frequency Research Scientist. During my 8.5 years with QinetiQ I worked across small and large research projects, gathering many skills along the way.

I have experience in data analysis (R Programming/Matlab), laboratory automation (LabVIEW) and electromagnetic simulation (CST Studio). The majority of my experience is in Windows but I have worked in Linux and am fairly familiar with how it functions. The laboratory research involved moving relatively large items so I have experience in complex multi-person lifting maneuvers of static and slung loads using forklifts and telehandlers, aswell as using mobile elevated working platforms.I have done a great deal of liasing with other disciplines such as engineering, electronics, IT and security to ensure we have the correct expertise feeding into projects. For nine months I took on the role of team leader where I led a group of eleven scientists and engineers guiding them through their personal development reviews. This was a sharp learning curve for myself as previously I had been heavily technical and the people management was an entirely new ball game. I fully commited to the role and received outstanding feedback from members of the team on my performance.

In my spare time I have been looking into C++ programming and physical simulations/animations. Through reading, learning, tutorials and a bit of trial'n error I have taught myself how to programme several small programmes in C++. I found investigating the computational methods to create an output to be engaging and interesting. Please see above some of the simulations in question and feel free to message me about any of them.
